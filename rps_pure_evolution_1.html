Simulation Rules

Population: N = 900 players (300 Rock, 300 Paper, 300 Scissors initially)

Each generation: players are randomly paired into 450 pairs.

Outcome Offspring (next generation)
Win2 copies of winner's type, 0 for loser
Tie1 copy each → net unchanged

Population size remains fixed at N = 900 each generation.

Dominance Cycle

Rock ≻ Scissors ≻ Paper ≻ Rock

Because no strategy is unconditionally dominant, the population cycles rather than converging to a pure Nash Equilibrium. Without mutation it eventually fixes by drift; with mutation it cycles indefinitely — matching predictions from evolutionary game theory.

Key Result

The cyclic dominance means that any pure strategy that becomes dominant eventually creates pressure for the strategy that beats it. This prevents stable equilibrium with pure strategies — the system only stabilises at the mixed NE (1/3, 1/3, 1/3).
